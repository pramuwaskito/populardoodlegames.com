Google Doodle Games are interactive games that appear on the Google homepage as part of their "Doodle" feature. These games are typically created to celebrate special occasions, events, or anniversaries, often celebrating events, anniversaries, or famous individuals, and are designed to be both fun and educational. When you visit Google on a day when a Doodle game is active, you'll see a playable game instead of the regular Google logo.

Here are some popular Google Doodle games that have been widely enjoyed over the years, but many are still playable today via the Google Doodles Archive :

Popular Google Doodle Games:

1. Pac-Man (2010)

  • Celebrated the 30th anniversary of Pac-Man.
  • A fully playable version of the arcade classic, complete with the original maze.
  • Fun fact: It was Google's first-ever playable Doodle.

2. Doodle Champion Island Games (2021)

  • Released for the Tokyo Olympics.
  • A large, story-driven sports RPG-style game.
  • Features mini-games like table tennis, archery, skateboarding, and more.
  • Played as Lucky the Ninja Cat.

3. Coding for Carrots (2017)

  • Celebrated 50 years of kids coding languages.
  • Puzzle game where you help a rabbit collect carrots using coding blocks.

4. Halloween Series

  • 2016: Played as a cat named Momo casting spells against ghosts.
  • 2020: Multiplayer game where players work together as ghosts to collect flames.
  • 2022: This Halloween revisiting an old haunt, but with some extra flare.
  • 2024: Help Momo the cat battle ghostly opponents across the atmosphere.

5. Basketball (2012)

  • Simple and addictive basketball shooting game.
  • Part of a sports-themed series during the 2012 Olympics.

6. Cricket (2017)

  • Released during the ICC Champions Trophy.
  • Fun and cute — you play as a cricket batting against snails.

7. Garden Gnomes (2018)

  • Catapult gnomes as far as possible to plant flowers.
  • Introduces a bit of German folklore and history.

8. Pony Express (2015)

  • Ride across the Wild West collecting mail and avoiding obstacles.
  • Celebrated the 155th anniversary of the Pony Express.

9. Scoville Game (2016)

  • Learn about chili peppers and Scoville heat levels while battling them with ice cream.

10. Lotería (2019)

  • A multiplayer game based on the traditional Mexican game similar to Bingo.
  • Play online with friends or random players.

These games often have a nostalgic or historical theme, and they allow users to engage in a brief but enjoyable experience directly through their browsers. You can play them for free and typically don't need to install anything.
