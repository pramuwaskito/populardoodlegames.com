If you’re feeling bored or want a distraction after focusing on your work, Here are some popular Google Doodle games that have been widely enjoyed over the years, but many are still playable today by visiting the official Google Doodle Archive online at Google Doodles Archive.
Some of the most popular options range from classic arcade throwbacks to unique, themed adventures and memorable interactive Google Doodle games enjoyed over the years include:

1. Pac-Man (2010)

The first-ever playable Google Doodle, this fully functional version of the iconic arcade game lets you navigate the maze using your arrow keys, gobbling up dots and avoiding ghosts. Released on May 21, 2010, to celebrate the game's 30th anniversary, the interactive doodle allowed users to:
  • Navigate the maze using their arrow keys (Player 1) or WASD keys (Player 2, if a second "coin" was inserted).
  • Gobble up dots (pellets) and power pellets.
  • Avoid the four ghosts: Blinky, Pinky, Inky, and Clyde.

2. Doodle Champion Island Games (2021)

One of Google's most extensive doodles, this is a full-fledged anime-style sports role-playing game where you play as a cat named Lucky competing in seven different sports to collect sacred scrolls.
  • Released for the Tokyo Olympics.
  • A large, story-driven sports RPG-style game.
  • Features mini-games like table tennis, archery, skateboarding, and more.
  • Played as Lucky the Ninja Cat.

3. Coding for Carrots (2017)

This interactive Doodle celebrates 50 years since kids programming languages were first introduced to the world.
  • The game uses a visual, block-based coding system, making it accessible for beginners.
  • Puzzle game where you help a rabbit collect carrots using coding blocks.
  • It consists of six levels that increase in difficulty, introducing more complex concepts like loops in later stages.
  • The goal was to introduce a large audience to the basics of coding in a fun and engaging way.

4. Halloween Series (Magic Cat Academy)

In this game, a magic cat named Momo fights ghosts by having the player draw shapes and symbols (lines, V-shapes, etc.) with their mouse or finger on the screen.
  • 2016: Played as a cat named Momo casting spells against ghosts.
  • 2020: Multiplayer game where players work together as ghosts to collect flames.
  • 2022: This Halloween revisiting an old haunt, but with some extra flare.
  • 2024: Help Momo the cat battle ghostly opponents across the atmosphere.

5. Basketball (2012)

Players used either the space bar or the mouse button to control a character and shoot as many baskets as possible within a 24-second limit, which is the standard shot clock time in basketball.
  • Simple and addictive basketball shooting game.
  • Part of a sports-themed series during the 2012 Olympics.

6. Cricket (2017)

A simple yet addictive game where you play as a cricket and have to time your swings to hit the ball and score runs.
  • Released during the ICC Champions Trophy.
  • Fun and cute — you play as a cricket batting against snails.

7. Garden Gnomes (2018)

To celebrate Garden Day in Germany, this game involves launching gnomes from a trebuchet to see how far they can fly and plant flowers.
  • Catapult gnomes as far as possible to plant flowers.
  • Introduces a bit of German folklore and history.

8. Pony Express (2015)

Marking the 155th anniversary of the mail service, players use arrow keys to navigate a horse and rider across the Old West, collecting mail and dodging obstacles like cacti and rocks.
  • Ride across the Wild West collecting mail and avoiding obstacles.
  • Celebrated the 155th anniversary of the Pony Express.

9. Birth of Hip Hop(2017)

This musical doodle celebrates the birth of hip-hop by providing interactive turntables where users can mix samples from classic tracks and allowed users to engage in activities like DJing and learning about the four elements of hip hop.
  • The game is divided into several mini-games where you can scratch records, create graffiti art, and perform breakdancing moves.
  • It educates players about the history of hip hop, including its founding pioneers, through animations and narration by hip hop icon Fab 5 Freddy.

10. Lotería (2019)

This digital version of the traditional Mexican card game allows for multiplayer sessions against friends or random opponents, playing much like bingo.
  • A multiplayer game based on the traditional Mexican game similar to Bingo.
  • Play online with friends or random players.
